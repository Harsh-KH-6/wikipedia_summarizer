Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide a better user experience.

2. How We Use Cookies

Wikipedia Summarizer uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function properly
  • Preference cookies: Remember your settings and preferences
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable basic functions like page navigation and access to secure areas of the website.

3.2 Preference Cookies

These cookies enable the website to remember information that changes the way the website behaves or looks, like your preferred language or the region you are in.

3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third-party services that appear on our pages. We use the following third-party services that may set cookies:

  • Wikipedia API (for content delivery)
  • GitHub (for issue tracking)

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish. You can delete all cookies that are already on your computer and you can set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. However, if you do this, you may have to manually adjust some preferences every time you visit our website.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update our Cookie Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Cookie Policy on this page.
